# Tools & Technologies

> What tools are used in personal training for overweight clients and how they fit together.

---

## Client Management & Programming Software

| Tool | Purpose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Hevy Coach** | Workout programming, progress tracking, client management | Clients can log workouts, upload progress photos, track body composition. Trainer sees everything in a dashboard. |
| **TrainerMetrics** | Fitness assessment & tracking | 90+ peer-reviewed assessments, body scanner integration. Standardized testing protocols. Good for data-driven trainers. |
| **TrueCoach** | Program delivery, client communication, progress tracking | Popular with independent trainers. Clean interface for assigning workouts and tracking compliance. |
| **FitSW** | Client management, workout tracking, nutrition logging | All-in-one platform. Includes progress photos, goal setting, and habit tracking. |
| **MevoLife** | All-in-one coaching platform | Tracks BMI, BMR, daily calorie requirements, ideal weight. Automated follow-ups. |
| **Trainerize** | Online training platform, workout delivery, habit coaching | Strong for hybrid (in-person + online) training. Integrates with MyFitnessPal. |
| **Exercise.com** | Business management + client training platform | Scheduling, payments, workout delivery, progress tracking in one platform. |
| **Google Sheets / Excel** | Manual program design and tracking | Still widely used by many trainers. Free, flexible, but requires manual data entry. |

## Assessment & Measurement Tools

| Tool | Purpose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Bioimpedance scale (Tanita, InBody)** | Body composition measurement | Non-invasive. InBody is the gold standard for gym-based body comp. Results can vary with hydration. |
| **Tape measure** | Circumference measurements | Waist, hips, chest, arms, thighs. Simple, reliable, no special equipment needed. Critical for tracking where the scale doesn't tell the full story. |
| **Skinfold calipers** | Body fat estimation | Requires training to use accurately. Can be uncomfortable or invasive for some clients — use with sensitivity and consent. |
| **Blood pressure cuff** | Resting blood pressure | Important baseline for clients with hypertension risk. Affordable and easy to use. |
| **Heart rate monitor (chest strap or watch-based)** | Exercise intensity monitoring | Polar, Garmin, Wahoo. Note: medications can alter heart rate response — use RPE as primary intensity measure for medicated clients. |
| **Grip strength dynamometer** | Baseline strength assessment | Quick, non-invasive. Grip strength correlates with overall health outcomes. |
| **Goniometer** | Joint range of motion measurement | Used for more detailed movement assessments. More common in clinical settings. |

## Client-Facing Tracking Tools

| Tool | Purpose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **MyFitnessPal** | Food logging & calorie tracking | Most widely used nutrition app. Integrates with many platforms. Use carefully — can trigger obsessive behavior in some clients. |
| **Cronometer** | Detailed nutrition tracking | More accurate micronutrient data than MFP. Preferred by nutrition-focused trainers. |
| **Fitbit / Apple Watch / Garmin** | Daily activity tracking (steps, heart rate, sleep) | Useful for encouraging non-exercise activity (step goals). Sleep data can be valuable for understanding recovery. |
| **Progress photo apps** | Standardized progress photos | Some built into platforms (Hevy Coach, TrueCoach). Consistent lighting/positioning matters. |

## Exercise Equipment (Overweight-Client Specific Considerations)

| Equipment | Use | Notes for Overweight Clients |
|---|---|---|
| **Sturdy box / bench** | Squat target, step-ups, seated exercises | Check weight rating. Use a solid platform, not a flimsy aerobic step. |
| **Cable machine** | Rows, presses, woodchops, pallof press | Versatile. Standing cable work avoids floor transitions. |
| **Leg press** | Lower body strength | Seated, supported. Easier on joints than barbell squats early on. Check seat width. |
| **Lat pulldown** | Upper body pull strength | Seated, supported. Alternative to pull-ups (which are unrealistic initially). |
| **Resistance bands** | Warm-up, activation, assisted movements | Low-cost, portable. Good for home exercise. Multiple resistance levels. |
| **TRX / Suspension trainer** | Scalable bodyweight exercises | Can adjust difficulty by changing body angle. Great for rows and assisted squats. |
| **Stationary bike (recumbent preferred)** | Low-impact cardio | Recumbent is easier to get on/off and supports the back. Check seat width. |
| **Treadmill** | Walking at incline | Walking, not running. Incline walking is excellent low-impact cardio. Ensure client is comfortable with the belt speed and has access to handrails. |
| **Rowing machine (Concept2)** | Full-body low-impact cardio | Good option but getting on/off can be difficult. Check seat width. |
| **Swimming pool** | Non-weight-bearing cardio and resistance | Excellent for clients with severe joint limitations. Requires pool access. Privacy can be a concern. |
| **Kettlebells** | Deadlifts, goblet squats, carries | Start light. Teach the hip hinge pattern. Farmer's carries are an excellent functional exercise. |

## Typical Tool Stack (Independent Trainer)

A solo trainer working with overweight clients would typically use:

1. **Programming & tracking:** Hevy Coach or TrueCoach (or Google Sheets)
2. **Scheduling:** Calendly or Trafft
3. **Payments:** Square or Stripe
4. **Assessment:** Tape measure + bioimpedance scale + blood pressure cuff
5. **Monitoring during sessions:** Heart rate monitor + RPE conversation
6. **Client communication:** WhatsApp + email
7. **Nutrition awareness (optional, within scope):** MyFitnessPal recommendation to client
