# Roles & Responsibilities

> Who does what in the domain of personal training for overweight clients.

---

## Primary Roles

### Personal Trainer (Generalist)

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Responsibilities** | Assess clients, design exercise programs, deliver training sessions, track progress, provide general wellness guidance, maintain client safety. |
| **Qualifications** | Nationally recognized certification (NASM-CPT, ACE-CPT, ACSM-CPT, NSCA-CSCS, ISSA-CPT). CPR/AED certification. Professional liability insurance. |
| **Reports to** | Gym manager (if employed) or self (if independent). |
| **Works with** | Clients, gym staff, front desk, other trainers, and potentially healthcare providers. |
| **Scope boundaries** | Cannot diagnose medical conditions, prescribe medical nutrition therapy, or provide mental health treatment. |

### Weight Management Specialist

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Responsibilities** | All of the above, plus: deeper behavior change coaching, nutritional habit guidance (within scope), coordination with healthcare team, specialized programming for obesity-related limitations. |
| **Qualifications** | Base PT certification + weight management specialization: ACE Weight Management Specialist, NASM Weight Loss Specialist (WLS), ISSA Weight Management Specialist, or AFPA Weight Management Specialist. |
| **Reports to** | Same as generalist. |
| **Works with** | Same as generalist, plus more frequent coordination with registered dietitians, physicians, and sometimes mental health professionals. |
| **Scope boundaries** | Same legal scope as generalist. Specialization provides deeper knowledge but does not expand legal scope of practice. |

## Supporting Roles (The Client's Extended Team)

These professionals are not personal trainers but frequently interact with this domain.

### Registered Dietitian (RD / RDN)

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Responsibilities** | Provide medical nutrition therapy, create individualized meal plans for medical conditions, manage nutrition for diabetes/cardiovascular disease/eating disorders, monitor bloodwork-related nutrition markers. |
| **Interaction with PT** | Receives referrals from PT. May co-manage clients. PT handles exercise, RD handles clinical nutrition. Should communicate regularly (with client consent) about shared clients. |
| **When to involve** | Client has diabetes, cardiovascular disease, eating disorder, food allergies requiring management, or needs a structured clinical meal plan. |

### Physician / Primary Care Doctor

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Responsibilities** | Medical clearance for exercise, monitoring comorbidities (hypertension, diabetes, cardiovascular disease), prescribing medication, ordering lab work, overseeing overall health. |
| **Interaction with PT** | Provides medical clearance. May refer patients to PT for exercise. PT refers clients back for medical concerns. |
| **When to involve** | Before starting any client with known medical conditions. When a client reports new symptoms. When progress stalls and underlying medical causes are suspected. |

### Physiotherapist / Physical Therapist

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Responsibilities** | Rehabilitate injuries, manage chronic pain, prescribe therapeutic exercise for specific conditions, perform clinical movement assessments. |
| **Interaction with PT** | May refer post-rehab clients to PT for ongoing fitness. PT refers clients with persistent pain or suspected injury. Some exercises may overlap — communication prevents contradictory programming. |
| **When to involve** | Client has persistent joint pain, recovering from surgery, or has a movement dysfunction beyond normal training scope. |

### Mental Health Professional (Psychologist / Counselor / Therapist)

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Responsibilities** | Treat depression, anxiety, body image disorders, disordered eating, trauma. Provide coping strategies and therapeutic support. |
| **Interaction with PT** | PT refers clients showing signs of mental health concerns. Therapist may encourage exercise as part of treatment plan. |
| **When to involve** | Client shows signs of disordered eating, severe body image distress, depression, anxiety that affects daily function, or discloses trauma related to weight/body. |

### Bariatric Surgeon / Bariatric Medicine Specialist

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Responsibilities** | Evaluate candidates for bariatric surgery, perform surgical procedures, manage post-surgical nutrition and activity protocols. |
| **Interaction with PT** | PT may work with pre-surgical clients to build fitness before surgery and post-surgical clients following surgeon-provided exercise timelines. Surgeon sets restrictions; PT programs within them. |
| **When to involve** | Client expresses interest in bariatric surgery or has already undergone the procedure. |

## Role in the Gym/Studio Ecosystem

### Gym Manager / Studio Owner

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Responsibilities** | Facility management, equipment procurement, hiring trainers, setting policies, marketing. |
| **Interaction with PT** | Sets training floor rules, equipment budgets, and client policies. A good manager ensures equipment is rated for heavier clients and the environment is inclusive. |

### Front Desk / Reception Staff

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Responsibilities** | Client greeting, scheduling, membership management. |
| **Interaction with PT** | First point of contact for clients. Their attitude sets the tone. Sensitivity training for front desk staff interacting with overweight clients matters. |

### Group Fitness Instructors

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Responsibilities** | Deliver group classes (yoga, spin, boot camp, etc.). |
| **Interaction with PT** | PT may refer clients to beginner-friendly group classes as supplementary activity. PT should know which instructors/classes are modification-friendly and welcoming to larger bodies. |

## Certification Landscape

| Organization | Base Certification | Weight Management Add-on | Cost Range (USD) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ACE** | ACE-CPT | Weight Management Specialist | $99-$199 (add-on) |
| **NASM** | NASM-CPT | Weight Loss Specialist (WLS) | $199-$399 (add-on) |
| **ISSA** | ISSA-CPT | Weight Management Specialist | ~$399 (add-on) |
| **AFPA** | AFPA-CPT | Weight Management Specialist | ~$399 (add-on) |
| **ACSM** | ACSM-CPT | No specific weight management add-on (covered in Exercise Physiologist track) | N/A |
| **NSCA** | NSCA-CSCS / NSCA-CPT | No specific weight management add-on (foundational knowledge included) | N/A |

All base certifications require CPR/AED certification and renewal through continuing education credits (CECs/CEUs) every 2-3 years.
